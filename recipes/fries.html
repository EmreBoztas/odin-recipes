French Fries Recipes

Short Description: Crispy on the outside, fluffy on the inside, homemade French fries are a classic comfort food. This recipe covers the essential steps to achieve perfect fries using the double-fry method for maximum crispiness.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (≈454 g) russet potatoes (about 2–3 medium potatoes)
  • Oil for deep frying (neutral, high-smoke-point oil such as canola, vegetable, or peanut) — about 4 cups (≈950 ml) or enough to fill your fryer/pot for deep frying
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional: black pepper, garlic powder, paprika, or fresh herbs for finishing

Equipment

  • Large pot or deep fryer
  • Slotted spoon or spider/skimmer
  • Paper towels or a cooling rack
  • Large bowl and clean kitchen towel or paper towels for drying

Steps

  1. Prep the potatoes

    1. Peel (optional) and cut potatoes into even sticks — about 1/3–1/2 inch (8–12 mm) thick.
    2. Place the cut potatoes in a large bowl of cold water and soak for 20–30 minutes (or up to 2 hours). This removes excess starch and helps them crisp.

  2. Dry the potatoes

    Drain the potatoes and rinse under cold water. Spread them out on a clean kitchen towel or paper towels and pat dry thoroughly. Removing moisture is key to crispiness.

  3. First fry (blanching)

    Heat oil to 325°F (≈163°C). Fry the potatoes in batches (do not overcrowd) until they are soft but not browned — about 4–6 minutes per batch. Remove and drain on paper towels or a rack. Let them cool for 10–15 minutes.

  4. Second fry (crisping)

    Increase oil temperature to 375°F (≈191°C) (you can go up to 400°F / ≈204°C for extra crisp). Fry the cooled potatoes again in batches until golden brown and crisp — about 2–4 minutes. Remove, drain, and immediately season with salt.

  5. Serve

    Serve hot. Add any finishing seasonings (pepper, paprika, garlic powder, grated Parmesan, chopped parsley, etc.) right after frying so they stick.

Tips & Variations

  • Cut Size Matters: Thicker fries (steak fries) take longer; shoestrings crisp faster. Keep pieces uniform for even cooking.
  • Longer For Extra Crispiness: A 1–2 hour soak (or overnight in the fridge) improves texture.
  • Baked Alternative: Toss potato sticks with a little oil, spread on a tray, and bake at 425°F (≈218°C), turning once, until golden (approx. 25–35 minutes). They’ll be less deep-fried crispy but still tasty.
  • Seasoning Ideas: Truffle oil & parmesan, Cajun spice, garlic & rosemary, or classic vinegar & salt (British style).
