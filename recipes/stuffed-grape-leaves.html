Stuffed Grape Leaves Recipes

Short Description: Stuffed grape leaves, also known as dolmas or sarma, are a popular dish in Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisines. This recipe provides a step-by-step guide to making delicious vegetarian stuffed grape leaves filled with a flavorful mixture of rice, herbs, and spices.

Ingredients (makes ~30–40 small rolls / 4 servings)

Filling

  • 1 cup (200 g) short-grain rice, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 6 tbsp (90 ml) olive oil
  • 2 tbsp pine nuts (optional)
  • 2 tbsp currants or raisins (optional)
  • 1–2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • ½ tsp ground allspice (optional)
  • 1 large bunch parsley, finely chopped (about 1 cup / 25 g)
  • 4–5 fresh mint leaves, chopped (or 1 tsp dried mint)
  • Juice of 1 lemon (for filling)

Leaves & cooking

  • ~50 jarred grape leaves (in brine), drained and rinsed — or fresh leaves, blanched
  • 3–4 tbsp olive oil (for pot and finishing)
  • Juice of 1–2 lemons (for cooking liquid)
  • Water or light vegetable/chicken stock to cover the rolls
  • Lemon slices (optional, for layering)

Equipment

  • Large sauté pan or skillet
  • Large pot with a tight-fitting lid (wide and shallow is best)
  • Mixing bowl
  • Slotted spoon or tongs
  • Small plate (to press rolls while cooking) or heatproof weight
  • Sharp knife and chopping board

Steps (step-by-step)

  1. Prepare the leaves

    1. If using jarred grape leaves: rinse well under cold water to remove brine and drain. Pat dry.
    2. If using fresh: blanch leaves in boiling water 1–2 minutes until soft, then transfer to ice water and drain.

  2. The filling

    1. 4 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté the chopped onion until soft and translucent (4–6 min).
    2. Add pine nuts and sauté 1–2 minutes until slightly toasted.
    3. Stir in tomato paste and cook 1 minute.
    4. Add the rinsed rice, salt, pepper, and allspice; toss to coat the rice with the oil and paste.
    5. Pour in 1 cup (240 ml) water (or a little less) — just enough to start softening the rice — bring to a simmer, cover, and cook gently for 8–10 minutes until the rice is partly absorbed but still firmer than fully cooked (it will finish cooking inside the leaves).
    6. Remove from heat; stir in chopped parsley, mint, currants (if using), and lemon juice. Let the filling cool until easy to handle.

  3. Roll the leaves

    1. Lay a grape leaf glossy side down, stem end facing you. Trim the stem if long.
    2. Place ~1–1½ tsp (for small rolls) to 1 tbsp (for larger rolls) of filling near the stem, centered horizontally but a bit below center.
    3. Fold the bottom edge up over the filling, fold both sides toward the center, then roll tightly away from you to form a cigar-shaped parcel. Keep them snug but don’t overpack — rice expands. Place seam-side down. Repeat until filling is used.

  4. Assemble in the pot

    1. Spread a few torn or whole grape leaves on the bottom of the pot (this prevents sticking) and add a few lemon slices.
    2. Pack the rolls seam-side down in tight concentric circles or rows. Place them snugly — this helps them keep shape while cooking.
    3. Drizzle 2–3 tbsp olive oil and the juice of 1 lemon over the rolls. Add enough water or stock to cover the rolls by about 1 cm (½ inch). If needed, place a small heatproof plate on top of the rolls to keep them submerged.

  5. Cook

    1. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, then reduce to low, cover, and cook for 30–45 minutes (vegetarian) or 45–60 minutes (meat variation), until rice and meat (if used) are fully cooked. Check liquid level midway; add a little hot water if it looks dry.

  6. Rest & serve

    1. Turn off heat and let the pot sit, covered, for 10–15 minutes. This firms the rolls and settles flavors.
    2. Serve warm or at room temperature with plain yogurt, lemon wedges, and extra olive oil.
